Biography

Beverly Heather D’Angelo (born November 15, 1951) is an accomplished and celebrated actress from the United States. Her career trajectory skyrocketed during the early 1980s when she snagged the coveted role of Ellen Griswold in the iconic National Lampoon’s Vacation films.

Her initial foray into the film world came with the 1977 release of the classic Annie Hall. From there, she went on to captivate audiences with her performances in numerous other films, such as First Love, Paternity, and Highpoint. Additionally, D’Angelo has graced television screens in various shows, including Doubletake and Slow Burn.

With her magnetic presence and incredible acting abilities, it’s no wonder that Beverly D’Angelo is a sought-after actress who has undeniably impacted the industry.

Early Life & Education

Beverly D’Angelo, an accomplished and versatile actress, was born on November 15, 1951, in Columbus, Ohio, United States. She was born to Gene D’Angelo, a bass player and television station manager at WBNS-TV, and Priscilla D’Angelo, a talented violinist.

Beverly‘s father hailed from an Italian heritage, imbuing her with a unique multi-racial identity that has influenced her life and career. She was the only girl growing up among three brothers, Tony D’Angelo, Jeff D’Angelo, and Tim D’Angelo, with whom she shared a close bond. Her grandfather is Howard Dwight Smith, a renowned architect.

With an early passion for acting, she displayed her talent in numerous school plays and eventually attended Upper Arlington High School in Ohio. Beverly D’Angelo‘s remarkable achievements in acting led to the presentation of an Upper Arlington Alumni Award (UAAA) in 2009, a testament to her exceptional performance and influence in the entertainment industry.

Personal Life

Beverly D’Angelo, an accomplished actress, and multi-faceted personality, has had an eventful personal life as well. In 1981, she tied the knot with Don Lorenzo Salviati, an Italian gentleman who was the sole heir of Don Forese Salviati. The union, however, did not last, and the two separated after 14 years of marriage.

After her split from Lorenzo, Beverly started seeing Anton Furst, a celebrated production designer, in 1987. Their relationship was tragically cut short in 1991 when Anton took his own life, leaving Beverly devastated.

Years later, Beverly found love again and began dating the iconic actor Al Pacino in 1997. The couple welcomed twins, a son, Anton James Pacino, and a daughter, Olivia Pacino, through IVF on January 5, 2001. Sadly, their relationship also came to an end in 2003.

Before these high-profile relationships, Beverly D’Angelo had also dated a number of other notable figures, including Milos Forman, Neil Jordan, and Damian Chapa. She had also been in relationships with Scott Baio and Sam Kinison.

Career

Beverly D’Angelo found her footing in the entertainment industry after an impressive debut on Broadway in 1976. After her minor appearance in Annie Hall in 1977, she quickly emerged as a force to be reckoned with in Hollywood, showcasing her acting chops in numerous blockbusters of the late 70s, such as Hair, Coal Miner’s Daughter, and Every Which Way But Loose. Her role in the latter even earned her a Golden Globe nomination for Best Supporting Actress, cementing her status as a rising star.

Her role in the National Lampoon’s Vacation franchise propelled her to the top of the entertainment industry in 1983. Starring alongside Chevy Chase, D’Angelo‘s role as Ellen Griswold became a household name, appearing in four sequels and a short film between 1985 and 2015.

Throughout the 1980s, D’Angelo remained a fixture in the comedy scene, appearing in several major comedy films. However, as the mid-1990s approached, she began taking on more independent roles. In 1994, she returned to the stage and showcased her versatility by winning a Theatre World Award for her performance in the Off-Broadway play Simpatico.

Beverly D’Angelo‘s range as an actress was further highlighted by her Emmy Award nomination for her performance as Stella Kowalski in the 1984 TV movie adaptation of A Streetcar Named Desire. She continued to impress audiences with roles in several made-for-television dramatic films, including Slow Burn, Judgment Day: The John List Story, and Sweet Temptation. Beverly D’Angelo‘s exceptional acting talent and undeniable charm have made her a beloved icon in the entertainment industry.
