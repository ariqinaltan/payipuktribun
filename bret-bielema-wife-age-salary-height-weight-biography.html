When it comes to American college football, he’s a ringing coaching name. The name is Bret Bielema. Bret played college football as a defensive lineman at the University of Iowa from 1989 to 1992 under coach Hayden Fry and served as team captain during his senior season. He also played for an Arena Football League team, the Milwaukee Mustangs.

Bret Bielema Biography – Age

Bret Bielema was born Bret Arnold Bielema on January 13 , 1970, to his parents – Arnie Bielema (father) and Marilyn Bielema (mother). Her two sisters are Brandi Bielema and Betsy Bielema. Bret is originally from Prophetstown, Illinois. There is no information regarding the high school he attended, but for college, he attended the University of Iowa where he earned a bachelor’s degree in marketing. He was the head football coach at the University of Wisconsin, Madison from 2006 to 2012.

Career

In his first season as head coach of the Wisconsin Badgers, Bret Bielema’s team ended the regular season 11-1 (7-1 in Big Ten Conference play) with a 14-0 victory over State. of San Diego on September 16, 2006. This made Bret the third Wisconsin coach to win the first three games of his career. In November 2006, with a 24-21 victory over the Iowa Hawkeyes, Bret Bielema became the first head coach in Big Ten history to win ten games in his first season.

Bret was named a finalist for the 2010 Bear Bryant Award, an award given to football’s coach of the year. He, however, lost the award to Auburn’s Gene Chizik. Bret Bielema is the only coach in Wisconsin history to have lost consecutive Rose Bowls. On December 4, 2012, Bret announced that he was leaving Wisconsin to become the head coach of the Arkansas Razorbacks (University of Arkansas). He left partly because he felt his assistant coaches were not well paid and also because he wanted to play in the Southeastern Conference. His first season didn’t start very well and it was the Razorbacks’ worst Southeastern Conference record since joining in 1992.

There was some improvement in its second season. Bret won his first two Southeastern Conference games in dominant fashion. Although Arkansas lost its final conference games to Missouri, they were still the first unranked team in college football history to eliminate two consecutive ranked opponents. In its third season, the team got off to a slow start and suffered a few losses as well. They lost to Toledo and Texas Tech in the non-conference.
