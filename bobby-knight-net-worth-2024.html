Coaching Career

Bobby Knight’s coaching career is the primary source of his wealth. He started his coaching career at Army before moving to Indiana University, where he achieved his greatest success. His tenure at Indiana University lasted for 29 years, during which he led the team to three NCAA championships, earning him a significant salary and bonuses.

Post-Indiana Career

After leaving Indiana, Knight continued his coaching career at Texas Tech, further adding to his wealth. Although his time at Texas Tech didn’t yield any NCAA championships, he still managed to lead the team to the NCAA tournament four times.

Broadcasting Career

Following his retirement from coaching, Knight transitioned into broadcasting. He worked as a studio analyst and game commentator for ESPN, providing insights based on his extensive coaching experience. His broadcasting career not only kept him in the public eye but also contributed to his net worth.

Authoring and Public Speaking

Bobby Knight is also a successful author. His books, including his autobiography “Knight: My Story,” have sold well, adding to his income. Additionally, Knight is a sought-after public speaker, commanding high fees for his appearances at corporate events, sports clinics, and other venues.

Charitable Work
