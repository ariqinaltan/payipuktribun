Ramadan is the Muslim holy month, which will begin on Monday, March 11, or Tuesday, March 12. The sighting of the new moon would be the determining factor.

The dawn-to-dusk fasting lasts between 12-17 hours anywhere, based on the side of the world in which the individual resides.

According to Islamic tradition, it is believed that Ramadan was the month when the first verse in the Holy Quran was revealed to the religion's Prophet Muhammad over 1,400 years ago.

Why Ramadan starts on different dates yearly

The differences between the year of one Ramadan and another have always been between 10-12 years.

This is due to the fact that the Islamic calendar is based on the lunar Hijri calendar and the months are either 29 or 30 days long.

The lunar calendar is shorter than the solar year for at least 11 days.

In the year 2030, Ramadan will be observed two times. The first one will be on January 5, while the second one will be on December 26.
