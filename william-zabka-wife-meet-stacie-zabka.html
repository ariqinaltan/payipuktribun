William Zabka is a famous American actor, producer, and martial artist who gained fame for his portrayal of Johnny Lawrence in the iconic 1984 film “The Karate Kid” and its sequels.

Born on October 20, 1965, in New York City, Zabka began his career in the entertainment industry at a young age and has since become a recognizable figure in the world of film and television.

Zabka’s breakthrough role came with “The Karate Kid,” where he played the role of the antagonist, Johnny Lawrence, a skilled and ruthless martial artist.

His portrayal of Johnny Lawrence earned him widespread acclaim, and the character has become one of the most memorable villains in movie history.

Zabka’s performance showcased his acting skills and his ability to bring depth and complexity to a seemingly one-dimensional character.

Following the success of “The Karate Kid,” Zabka continued to work in film and television, appearing in projects such as “Just One of the Guys,” “Back to School,” and “Crossing the Bridge.”

However, despite his talent and success, Zabka found it challenging to escape the shadow of his iconic character. He took a step back from acting for a while and focused on other pursuits.

In addition to his acting career, Zabka has been involved in martial arts for most of his life. He began studying Tang Soo Do at a young age and eventually earned a second-degree green belt.

This background in martial arts undoubtedly contributed to his authenticity in portraying Johnny Lawrence and his impressive on-screen fight scenes.

In recent years, Zabka experienced a resurgence in popularity and recognition, thanks to the revival of “The Karate Kid” franchise in the form of the television series “Cobra Kai.”

The show, which premiered in 2018, picks up decades after the events of the original films and follows the lives of Johnny Lawrence and his rival, Daniel LaRusso (played by Ralph Macchio).

Zabka reprised his role as Johnny Lawrence and also served as an executive producer on the series.
