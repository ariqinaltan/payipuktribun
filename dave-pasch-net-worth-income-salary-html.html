Dave Pasch is a sportscaster from the United States. Template:As of the date Pasch is a play-by-play announcer for ESPN, where he calls college football, college basketball, NBA, WNBA, and Arena football events. Pasch provided play-by-play commentary for ESPN Radio’s coverage of the 2013 Rose Bowl, which took place in Pasadena, California, on New Year’s Day, 201Pasch worked for ESPN in 2007 and called the Little League World Series. In addition to his work for ESPN, Pasch has worked for the West Virginia Radio Corporation, which broadcasts high school football, CBS Radio/Westwood One for golf, the NFL, and college basketball tournaments, and Fox Sports, which broadcasts football in Europe and the NFL, the Detroit Vipers, and the Chicago Blackhawks of the NHL. As a play-by-play broadcaster for the Arizona Cardinals since 2002, Pasch has collaborated with colour commentator and former NFL offensive fullback Ron Wolfley to provide the team’s broadcasts. Prior to joining the Arizona Cardinals Radio Network, Pasch served as the voice of the Syracuse Orange’s collegiate football and basketball teams for many years. Pasch has collaborated with a number of other analysts, including Rod Gilmore, Brian Griese, Trevor Matich, Doris Burke, Chris Spielman, Bill Walton, and Wolfley.
