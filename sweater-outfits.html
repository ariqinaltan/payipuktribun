12 Sweater Outfits That Feel Fresh and Fashion Forward

Publish date: 2024-08-22

When it comes to dressing for the cold weather, a classic chunky knit is essential (it's called sweater weather for a reason, is it not?). However, a sweater outfit can also come in handy on an early spring morning or on those cool summer nights. Not only is this wardrobe staple warm and cozy, but it's versatile enough to work for nearly every occasion, regardless of the season. Whether you're dressing up for a fall date night or aiming for a casual everyday look that still feels elevated and fun, there's no shortage of ways to style a sweater.

Our goal is to get creative with how to wear this piece, so we turned to street style stars as our sartorial guides. Ahead, we've rounded up our favorite sweater outfit ideas that delight and inspire. Aside from timeless combinations, we've included a few dressed-up solutions and celebrity-approved ideas in the mix. Who knew there were so many ways to wear this seasonal staple?

Keep scrolling for 12 sweater outfits that are worth adding to your rotation.

With Jeans

It's the easy outfit we daydream about on a particularly chilly day: a simple sweater and jeans pairing. This look never goes out of style because it's made up of foundational wardrobe staples. Curate a look that's all your own by playing with statement accessories, like layered necklaces and hats, and experimenting with the season's best shoe trends.

Under Overalls

Though some may consider it a summer garment (particularly when styled with a crop top or tank), overalls are actually seasonless. Throw a pair over an oversized sweater for a quick style solution. You can dress up the look with loafers or heeled boots and even experiment with outerwear (everything from a leather jacket to a tailored blazer is fair game). If you want to keep things casual, go for sneakers instead.

With a Skirt

Moody colors may have traditionally been popular for fall (okay, they still are), but why not experiment with a brighter palette, too? Starting with a multicolored sweater, find a bottom — maxi skirt, pleated mini skirt, jeans, etc. —in a color that's echoed in your top for the perfect accent.

To Pull Off Prep

For those who lean preppy, pair your sweater with colorful shorts and the trending sock-and-shoe combo for the ultimate light academia look. Added bonus: It will make you feel like a modern-day Carlton Banks — and yes, that's a good thing.

As a Dress

Sweater weather means never having to sacrifice style for comfort — especially since your favorite oversized knit can likely double as a dress. Embrace jellyfishing by pairing this piece with a form-fitting bottom half, whether that means adding some tights and a sleek pair of heels or opting for knee-high boots.

With Leggings

If expertly styled, leggings can be both cozy and elevated. A longer sweater can be worn with high-waisted leggings and chunky socks for the ultimate off-duty look. On the other hand, faux-leather leggings will add some edge that contrasts nicely with an oversized knit.

Over a Button-Down

Channel your inner Gossip Girl by layering a crewneck sweater or cardigan over a crisp button-down. Fold or pop your collar, then scrunch the arms of your sweater so that the shirt peeks out at the wrists.

Over a Dress

To break up a flowy maxi dress — and add an extra layer of warmth — throw on a sweater. In terms of colors, you can't go wrong with neutrals. For example, a black-and-white knit will work regardless of the color(s) of the dress.

With Trousers

Over the past year, roomy dress pants have become a laid-back staple beloved by celebrities. While still sleek enough for the office, they're perfect for a more casual day — especially when worn with chunky loafers. No matter the color of your sweater, neutral beige or black dress pants will complete any look.

For Contrasting Texture

Bulky sweaters and form-fitting skirts are complementary. However, throw in some contrasting texture and a monochrome color palette, and you've got yourself an outfit that's swoonworthy. For some variations here, don't be afraid to experiment with different hem lengths. While a maxi skirt is a fall staple, a mini skirt paired with knee-high boots — or socks — is another strong contender.

With Sweatpants

For days spent chilling on the couch, sweaters and sweatpants are a match made in loungewear heaven. Snuggle into your favorite pullover and ditch your jeans in favor of baggy joggers. Mix and match colors for a fun flair.

Tied Up

If your bulky sweater is feeling a little too, well, bulky, here's a style tip: Tie it up. By gathering the front of your sweater into a knot, you create a more form-fitting silhouette, which can be especially helpful for petite figures.
