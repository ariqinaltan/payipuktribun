Possible Malaysian Plane MH370 Debris Photos

Publish date: 2024-08-25

Search of Malaysian plane has reached one of its high points, as Australian satellite released images of possible debris.

Right now there is 1 airchaft searching the area, and 3 will join later today to locate the probable debris and either confirm or deny the theory.

According to Australia's Prime Minister Tony Abbott "New and credible information has come to light in relation to the search," he said during a regular sitting of Parliament.

Previous leads – like unidentified objects in the South China Sea, found by the Chinese satellite a week ago, or an oil slick by Vietnam that turned out to be a slick from local ships, both lead to nowhere.

According to the Prime Minister of Australia, all the data and the photos were handed to the Prime Minister of Malaysia as soon as they were discovered.

One of the aircrafts at the scene, PC-3 Orion has not spotted any debris yet. Three other planes – one from the US, one from New Zealand and a local merchant ship will arrive later today. Australia also sent a warship to this zone to help recover any large items, if found.There were two objects spotted by the satellite, one of which is believed to be the tail and the other - a part of the fuselage.

The aircrafts say that the weather and poor visibility may obstruct the search, and still there is no official confirmation whether these objects are connected with the aircraft.

Meanwhile, the search continues and some of the relatives of missing passengers are feeling too tired and stressed, they decided to not hear anything related with this search until there is some official information.

People all over the world are praying and still have hope to find the passengers alive.
