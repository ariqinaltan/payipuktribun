So You Wanna Trade Lobs for Lobsters? Airlines to Get You From Portland to Myrtle Beach

Let's face it, Maine is darn tootin' delightful. But who says you can't crave some sunshine and fried shrimp after a summer of chowdah and kayaking? Myrtle Beach beckons with its warm waves and putt-putt courses, and you, my friend, have the travel itch. But fear not, because navigating the airline jungle from Portland (PWM) to Myrtle Beach (MYR) is easier than navigating a lobster trap (although that's not saying much).

Taking Flight: Your Myrtle Beach Airline Options

Here's the lowdown on the airlines that can whisk you away from crustacean coves to Carolina shores:

  • The Big Three: United, Delta, and American Airlines are all solid choices, offering ample flights and a variety of connections. Think of them as the reliable uncles who always show up to the family reunion with slightly stale chips and dip, but you know they'll get you there.

  • Breeze Airways: This new kid on the block is shaking things up with potential for budget-friendly fares. Just like that surprise cousin who shows up fashionably late with a suitcase full of hilarious party games, Breeze Airways could be your wild card for a fun and affordable adventure.

  • Southwest Airlines: Southwest is known for its two-bag-free checked policy (that's right, folks, bring those extra pairs of flip flops!), and their friendly flight attendants are like that cool aunt who always has a story about a time she met a movie star.

Pro Tip: Play the Points Game

Frequent flier? This could be your chance to rack up some serious miles or points. Do a little research to see if your preferred airline offers a good route from PWM to MYR. Hey, a free flight to Hawaii next summer could be just a few beach trips away!

So You've Booked Your Flight, Now What?

Here comes the fun part: planning your Myrtle Beach itinerary! From indulging in endless buffets to conquering arcades and soaking up the sun, this vacation is your oyster (or, well, maybe a giant Carolina beach house shrimp).

Important: Don't forget to pack your sunscreen, swimsuit, and sense of adventure!

FAQ - Fly Like a Pro

How to find the cheapest flight?

Be flexible with your travel dates and consider flying on weekdays or during the shoulder seasons (spring and fall).

How to know how much luggage to pack?

Check the baggage allowance for your chosen airline. Remember, some airlines (like Southwest) include free checked bags, while others may charge extra.

How to score the best seat on the plane?

Book your flight in advance for the most selection. Consider upgrading to an aisle seat for extra legroom or a window seat for epic cloud gazing.

How to deal with layovers?

Pack a good book, download some podcasts, or unwind in the airport lounge (if you have access).

How to prepare for landing in Myrtle Beach?

Mentally switch gears from lighthouse spotting to seashell collecting. Your toes will be sinking into the sand in no time!
