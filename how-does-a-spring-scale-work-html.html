How does a spring scale work?

Publish date: 2024-08-24

Answer

It consists of a spring fixed at one end with a hook to attach an object at the other. It works by Hooke’s Law, which states that the force needed to extend a spring is proportional to the distance that spring is extended from its rest position. A spring scale cannot measure mass, only weight.

Also, what would you measure with a spring scale?

A spring scale, or a spring balance, is a device used to measure the weight of an object, not the mass of an object. An object’s mass refers the amount of matter in object. This number never changes. An object’s weight, however, is dependent on the amount of gravity that the object is experiencing.

Secondly, why is it important to hold the spring scale horizontal?

If you hold the scale perfectly horizontal, you measure the force necessary to overcome friction. If you hold the scale straight above the friction load, you will be measuring the force of its weight alone. There resultant makes an angle of 45 degrees to the greater force.

In this regard, are spring scales accurate?

Although spring scales are not expected to be as accurate as other types of scales, it still pays to know that you can measure items using this type of device with more-or-less the same accuracy as you would using a standard weighing scale.

How much is a spring scale?

Generally, small scales that measure newtons will have a less firm spring (one with a smaller spring constant) than larger ones that measure tens, hundreds or thousands of newtons or even more depending on the scale of newtons used. The largest spring scale ranged in measurement from 5000–8000 newtons.
